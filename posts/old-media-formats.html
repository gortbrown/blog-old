The Magic of Old Media Formats

I've always had a fond spot for old tech. There's something cool about seeing how tech worked and how people interacted with it that. And every time I see this old tech, I wonder what it would be like to use it. That's why I like projects like SpaceHey, which recreates MySpace, or NeoCities, which recreates the idea of GeoCitties. I've also found myself getting more into traditional media formats as well, like radio and traditional TV.

I'm on summer break right now, and I also work a remote IT job, meaning that I have a lot of time to kill while stuff isn't happening. This has mostly been filled up by streaming TV shows. Though as I've streamed more, I've gotten more bored. Sometimes you run out of stuff to watch, and having to find something to fill that gap can be a bit of a daunting task.

Of course, sometimes that just means it's time to switch over to music or podcasts, but sometimes you can fall into the same rut of trying to find something if you've listened to all of your podcasts or music. And of course, picking up a book and reading can solve this too, but there's also these older media formats to fall back on.

It's like TV, but the internet!

Everyone and their dog seems to have some sort of free TV-style streaming service nowadays. Samsung has one included with their TVs, SlingTV has a free option, Amazon Prime has FreeVee, etc. Seeing as my mom used to have a Sling subscription and the app was already on my TV, I used that.

I find it pretty nice to be able to go back to the channel format. Sure, you still have to choose what channel, but it allows you to just see what's on and choose based on that. It also gives me the chance to see shows I might not have been exposed to before. For example, I had never seen Alf before outside of one Mr. Robot episode, but I started watching a channel dedicated to Alf, and it's a pretty good show!

I've also started listening more to the radio, either through my car's radio, or through radio.net. I listened to the radio a lot as a kid in my parents' cars on the way to school, and on my radio alarm clock, so it felt nice to start listening to old stations I used to listen to, as well as some new ones in town since I no longer live in the same area I did all those years ago.

I think it's fun to look back on the tech that brought us to where we are today. I also think that sometimes it's nice to go back and use that old tech to take a breather from the issues with today's tech.
